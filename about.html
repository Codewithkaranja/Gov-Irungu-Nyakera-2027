Biography

Wilson Irungu Nyakera is a seasoned technocrat, entrepreneur, and public servant committed to building a Nairobi that works for everyone. Born and raised in Kenya, he has served in various key government positions, including Principal Secretary for Transport and Devolution, as well as leadership roles in public agencies like KEMSA and KICC.

As Nairobi gears up for the 2027 elections, Irungu is determined to transform our city into a hub of innovation, inclusivity, and sustainable development. His vision is rooted in transparency, accountability, and service-driven leadership that prioritizes the needs of all Nairobi residents.

Nyakera recently joined the Democracy for Citizens Party (DCP), which is associated with former Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, and serves as Nairobi County patron for the party. He has publicly signalled his intent to run for the Nairobi gubernatorial seat in the 2027 general election under the DCP banner.

Political Vision & Platform

Transforming Nairobi through accountable, innovative leadership

Governance Philosophy

Nyakera believes in citizen-centered governance where public officials are accountable servants, not masters. He advocates for transparency in county operations, participatory budgeting, and digital platforms for citizen engagement.

Nairobi's Challenges

He has criticized the current leadership over issues like service delivery, demolitions affecting livelihoods, and governance challenges. Nyakera positions himself as a candidate focused on practical solutions to Nairobi's most pressing problems.

2027 Gubernatorial Race

Early polling data suggests Nyakera is gaining attention in the Nairobi 2027 race. He seeks the DCP nomination for governor with a platform focused on accountability, development, and innovative solutions to urban challenges.

Values & Principles

Transparency, accountability, inclusivity, and service-driven leadership. Irungu believes that Nairobi's growth should benefit all its residents, not just a select few. He stands for democratic principles, having opposed moves like the impeachment of Governor Sakaja, asserting that democracy should be respected.
